      The Coroner: Inquest

      Case #{{ case_id }} — Briefing

      A body has been found. As the presiding Coroner, it falls to you to determine the cause and manner of death — and to name the person responsible. Evidence has been collected. Witnesses have been brought in for questioning. The truth is in the record. Find it.

      Your Investigation Tools
      • Overview — Read the case summary: victim profile, setting, and known timeline of events.
      • Witnesses — Interview each witness. Ask direct questions. They may lie, deflect, or reveal critical facts. Push harder if something doesn't add up.
      • Evidence — Examine all collected physical evidence. Click any item to view its forensic details and add annotations. Cite evidence you'll use in your report.
      • Consult — Call in Dr. Harlow (pathologist) or Dr. Avery (toxicologist) for specialist opinions on wounds, substances, and time of death.
      • Board — Use the free-draw investigation board to map connections, note suspects, and organize your theory visually. All pins save automatically.
      • Report — When ready, file your official Coroner's Report. Name the killer, cause of death, method, motive, and cite your supporting evidence. You get one submission per case.
      How You Are Scored
      • Correct perpetrator: 40 pts
      • Correct cause of death: 20 pts
      • Correct method: 15 pts
      • Correct motive: 10 pts
      • Key evidence cited: up to 15 pts
      Remember

      Witnesses can lie. Evidence can mislead. Your job is to weigh what you find and reach the most defensible verdict. Cases remain open until you file your report. There is no time limit — but there is only one submission.
